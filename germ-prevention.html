What Is Germ Prevention?

Germ prevention — also called disinfection service — is a targeted cleaning approach that focuses on eliminating bacteria, viruses, and other harmful microorganisms from your home or office. While regular cleaning removes visible dirt and grime, disinfection works at a deeper level to kill the pathogens that cause illness.

The most important areas to focus on are high-touch surfaces — doorknobs, light switches, cabinet pulls, countertops, remotes, phones, keyboards, and bathroom fixtures. These surfaces get touched dozens of times a day by multiple people, making them the fastest way germs spread through a household.

Germ Prevention FAQ

What's the difference between cleaning and disinfecting?

Cleaning removes visible dirt and residue from surfaces. Disinfecting kills bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens that you can't see. Both are important — cleaning first removes the layer that shields germs, then disinfecting eliminates what's underneath. Our germ prevention service does both.

How often should I schedule disinfection service?

During cold and flu season is the most common time, along with after someone in the household has been sick. Some families schedule it quarterly for peace of mind. We'll work with whatever schedule makes sense for your home and your health needs.

Is disinfecting safe for families with kids and pets?

Yes. The products we use are safe for families and pets once they've dried, which typically takes 15–30 minutes. We'll walk you through any precautions and let you know when it's safe to re-enter treated areas. We never use anything we wouldn't use in our own homes.

Can you come out quickly after someone gets sick?

We do our best to accommodate urgent situations. Give us a call at 785-452-0793 and we'll let you know our earliest availability. Illness in the home is stressful, and we want to help as quickly as we can.

Does germ prevention replace regular cleaning?

No — they work best together. Regular cleaning removes the dirt and grime that shields germs. Germ prevention then goes to work on the pathogens that remain. Think of it as an additional layer of protection on top of your regular cleaning schedule.
