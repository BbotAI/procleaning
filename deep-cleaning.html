What Makes a Deep Clean Different

Regular cleaning keeps up with the everyday. Deep cleaning catches everything that builds up underneath.

Even the most diligently maintained homes have areas that build up over time. Grease on the stove hood. Grime in the oven. Dust on the tops of cabinets. Buildup along baseboards and in the corners of tile. That's exactly what a deep clean is designed for.

A deep clean takes longer than a standard visit because we're going further. We move things to clean behind them. We open appliances and clean inside them. We get into the grout, the light fixtures, the ceiling fan blades — everything that makes a space feel truly clean, not just tidy.

Many new clients start with a deep clean before transitioning to a regular cleaning schedule. It creates a clean baseline, so ongoing visits can maintain it at a high level rather than playing catch-up.

Deep Cleaning Covers

  • All areas from standard cleaning, plus:
  • Inside the oven, refrigerator, and microwave
  • Behind and underneath appliances
  • Inside all cabinets and drawers
  • Grout scrubbing in bathrooms and kitchen
  • Baseboards, door frames, and moldings
  • Window sills, tracks, and interior glass
  • Ceiling fan blades and light fixture covers
  • Vents, switch plates, and outlet covers
  • Behind toilets, under sinks, and other hard-to-reach areas

When a Deep Clean Makes Sense

There are a few situations where a deep clean is exactly the right call.

Starting Fresh

New client, new home, or just a house that needs a reset — a deep clean gets you to a clean baseline before moving to a regular schedule.

Seasonal Cleaning

Spring cleaning, fall prep, or holiday-ready cleaning. Some things need more than a regular visit once or twice a year.

After Illness or Events

After a long illness, a big event at home, or extended time away — a deep clean brings the space back to its best.

Before Listing a Home

A deep clean before a home goes on the market makes a strong impression on buyers and can contribute to a higher selling price.

How Deep Cleaning Is Priced

Deep cleaning is priced based on the size of your home, its current condition, and which areas need the most attention. It will cost more than a standard visit because it simply takes more time — but it's an investment in your home that makes every visit after it easier and more efficient.

We give you a clear, honest quote before we start — no surprises, no hidden add-ons. If the job ends up being faster than expected, we don't pad the time. If something comes up mid-clean that we want to address, we'll ask you first.
