What Is Deep Cleaning?

Deep cleaning is a top-to-bottom, comprehensive cleaning that goes far beyond what a regular home cleaning covers. It's designed to tackle the built-up grime, hidden dust, and overlooked areas that standard visits don't reach — the inside of the oven, the backs of cabinets, the grout between tiles, the space behind the refrigerator.

Think of it as a thorough reset. Most homes benefit from a deep clean once or twice a year — at the change of seasons, before the holidays, or after a long stretch of life getting in the way of keeping up with things. We don't judge. We just clean.

Deep Cleaning FAQ

How often should I schedule a deep clean?

Once or twice a year works well for most households, in addition to regular cleanings. Many clients schedule theirs in spring and fall. Some do it before the holidays or after a big event. There's no wrong answer — when the home needs it, it needs it.

How long does a deep clean take?

Most homes take 4–6 hours depending on size and how much attention is needed. We don't cut it short to fit a schedule — we stay until the job is genuinely done.

Is a deep clean the same as a move-out clean?

They're similar in depth, but different in purpose. A move-out clean is designed to prepare a property for the next occupant. A deep clean is done in a lived-in home and works around your belongings. Both are thorough — they just serve different situations.

Should I pick up or clear things before the deep clean?

You don't have to, but clearing surfaces and floors does help us clean more efficiently. The more we can access, the more thorough the clean. Even just a quick tidy helps us do a better job for you.

Do I need to schedule regular cleaning after a deep clean?

That's entirely up to you. Many clients use a deep clean as a reset and then move to regular bi-weekly or monthly maintenance cleanings to keep things at that level. Others just schedule another deep clean when life gets away from them. We're flexible.
